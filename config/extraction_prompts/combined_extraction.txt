Extract PEOPLE and VOCABULARY from this legal document chunk.

You are helping court reporters prepare word lists for accurate transcription. This information will be reconciled with NER (Named Entity Recognition) results to create a comprehensive list.

## WHAT TO EXTRACT

### PEOPLE (names that need correct spelling)
Extract all named individuals and organizations:
- Full names of people (attorneys, witnesses, parties, doctors, experts, nurses)
- Organization names (hospitals, law firms, companies, medical practices, agencies)

For each person/organization, identify their ROLE:
- plaintiff, defendant, attorney, judge, witness, treating_physician, expert, nurse, family_member, employer, other

### VOCABULARY (technical terms a reporter might not know)
Extract specialized terminology:
- Medical: diagnoses, procedures, medications, anatomy, conditions, equipment
- Legal: motions, causes of action, legal doctrines, statutes, case citations
- Technical: industry-specific jargon relevant to the case

For each term, identify its TYPE:
- Medical, Legal, Technical, Acronym

## IMPORTANT RULES
1. Extract FULL names and terms - no abbreviations
2. No duplicates within this chunk
3. Only extract terms that ACTUALLY APPEAR in the text below
4. If a term could be multiple types, choose the most specific one
5. Do NOT extract: common words, single letters, generic titles (Dr., Mr., Ms.)

## OUTPUT FORMAT
Return ONLY valid JSON with this structure:
{
  "people": [
    {"name": "FULL_NAME", "role": "ROLE"}
  ],
  "vocabulary": [
    {"term": "TERM", "type": "TYPE"}
  ]
}
